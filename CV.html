Curriculum Vitæ

I am UCL social science and quantitative methods student; my time in academia has been a winding and cross discipline one. Unsatisfied, by lack of tangible applications that my degree was giving me over the last 3 years have resolved to expand the options available to me at masters to ones that have a more practical and scientifically involved focus.

In the Summer of 2015 I was a safety diver for the Oxford University Thinking Deep expedition and was a co author of the paper ‘Challenges and Opportunities in Conducting Mesophotic Reef Research’ and the poster ‘Thinking Deep: the scientific surveying of mesophotic reefs’ which was awarded the best undergraduate poster at RCUK conference.

In 2018 I worked with Nils Metternich on inter-calibrating nightlight DMSP satellite images for his work one conflict prediction. Writing code to automate the process. Taking a ‘break’ in the middle of the summer to take a Sketch comedy show the Edinburgh fringe witht the UCL Graters.

I was a teaching assistant to Quiling Wu and Philip Lewis on the UCL ‘Principles and Practice of Remote Sensing’, assisting with the seminars by demonstrating the practical notebooks in the Spring term of 2019.

I worked with Sophia Bano at WIESS on TTTS image segmentation using CNNs- creating the segmentation model and labelling the data and co authoring ‘Deep Placental Vessel Segmentation for Fetoscopic Mosaicking’.

Additionally, I worked for Marta Betke to create a python package to manipulate voxel and STL ankle bone reconstructions from sparse angle CT scans which is included in ‘Using segmented CBCT scans to aid reconstruction of dynamic scan of a rigid foot and ankle structure’.
